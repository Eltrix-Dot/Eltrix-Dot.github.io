📧 Email Masking: How Aliases Protect You

Rule: one service = one alias. This prevents cross-site tracking and makes breaches painless.

  1. Create one alias per site (ex: discord-9f3@yourdomain).
  2. Use that alias only for that one service.
  3. If the service is hacked or sells your address, disable/delete that alias.
  4. Your real inbox stays safe; the spam stops instantly.
Pro Tip: Combine with a password manager. Unique email + unique password = breach damage becomes minimal.

🧅 Tor & Anonymity (When VPN Isn't Enough)

For true anonymity: hide not just your traffic, but your identity and location from the sites you visit.

What's the difference between VPN and Tor?

VPN: Hides your traffic from your ISP and provides a different IP address. Good for privacy.

Tor: Routes traffic through multiple relays, hiding your real IP from destinations. Built for anonymity.

Reality check: Tor is slower by design. That's the trade-off for anonymity.

When should you use Tor?
  • When you need to hide your IP/location from the site you visit.
  • When you're researching sensitive topics.
  • When you want strong anti-tracking + anti-fingerprinting defaults.
Tor Safety Rules (Avoid Self-Doxxing)
  • Don't log into your real accounts (Google, personal email) in Tor.
  • Don't install extra extensions (fingerprinting risk).
  • Don't open downloaded documents while online (they can leak network info).
  • Keep Tor Browser updated.
Critical Rule: Anonymity breaks when you bring your real identity into the Tor session.
