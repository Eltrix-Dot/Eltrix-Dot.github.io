📧 Email Masking (The Power of Aliases)

Never give your real email

Anti-Breach

Your “main” inbox (for example Proton Mail) should be a vault: private, stable, and rarely shared. For everything else, use aliases (SimpleLogin).

Tuto: how aliases protect you
  1. Create one alias per site (ex: discord-9f3@yourdomain).
  2. Use that alias only for that one service.
  3. If the service is hacked or sells your address, disable/delete that alias.
  4. Your real inbox stays safe; the spam stops instantly.
Rule: one service = one alias. This prevents cross-site tracking and makes breaches painless.

🛡️ Interactive Hardening Guides

Quick Tips (click to expand)

10-Min Hardening
Browser: enable stronger tracking protection
Turn on Strict tracking protection (Firefox-based browsers) and disable unnecessary extensions.
Rule: fewer extensions = less fingerprint surface.
Search: replace Google
Switch to DuckDuckGo or (even better) SearXNG for metasearch without the same tracking incentives.
DNS: stop using your ISP DNS
Use privacy-respecting resolvers like Quad9 or NextDNS instead of your provider DNS.
Why: DNS is metadata. It reveals which domains you try to reach.
